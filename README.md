# Pizza Sales Data Analysis

## Overview
This project looks at pizza sales data to understand customer behavior, sales trends, and revenue patterns using Python. The main goal was to explore the data and find useful insights that could help explain what customers tend to order and when.

---

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## Key Insights
- The top five pizzas are very close in popularity, with sales ranging from 2,315 to 2,416 orders.
- Large pizzas are the most commonly ordered size, making up about 38.1% of all orders.
- Chicken pizzas bring in the highest average revenue at around $18.12.
- Orders tend to peak around lunch and dinner times.
- Most orders fall between $10 and $20.

---

## Visualizations
The project includes several visualizations to better understand the data:
- Bar charts for top-selling pizzas and revenue by category
- Pie chart for pizza size distribution
- Line chart showing orders by hour of day
- Scatterplot showing relationship between size and price
- Histogram of total order amounts

## Conclusion
Overall, the data shows that customers order a mix of different pizzas rather than focusing on just one type. Standard sizes (small, medium, large) make up most of the orders, and there are clear peaks in demand around lunch and dinner times. These patterns could be useful for planning inventory and staffing.
